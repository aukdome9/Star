# DAILY FLAME JOURNAL
### Companion Workbook to [[The Flame]]

*30 Days of Igniting, Tracking, and Embodying the Protocol of the Flame*

*You are not becoming disciplined. You are becoming coherent.*

---

## What It Is

A 30-day installation journal, not a collection of insights. Each day carries three sections — Morning Ignition (practice notes: intention, duration, what was felt), Throughout the Day Tracking (brief check-ins on where the flame stayed lit or flickered), and Evening Review (integration: what shifted, what to bring to the fire tomorrow).

## The Four-Week Arc

| Week | Focus | Core Question |
|---|---|---|
| One — Attention | Learning to see the flame clearly | Where do your attention leaks happen, and what quality (color) are you giving your focus? |
| Two — Life Force | Feeling and lifting the fuel | Where is life force spent unconsciously, and what does it feel like to lift rather than leak it? |
| Three — Focus & Intensity | Directing the forge | When does a situation call for a full blaze versus a steady, sustained burn? |
| Four — Embodiment | Becoming the flame | Did the flame feel like something visited, or something you were? |

Each week closes with an integration day (Days 7, 14, 21, 30) reviewing the biggest shift and naming what carries forward.

## Representative Prompts

- *Day 1:* What did you see/feel when you brought attention to your core? Where did the flame want to go first?
- *Day 8:* When you called on your life force to fuel the flame, what did you feel — surge, warmth, resistance?
- *Day 18:* You brought the flame to something ready to be purified or released. What burned? What remains?
- *Day 28:* Set an intention for reality reorganization. What evidence shows the flame is architecting your experience?
- *Day 30:* What does it mean now to say "I am the flame"? What is your commitment to the protocol going forward?

## Closing Line

*You did not complete a journal. You installed a new way of being. The fire does not end. It only transforms.*
