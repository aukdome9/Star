# Posting Cadence — Contract

> [!IMPORTANT]
> This is the standing rule for X (Twitter). Every 30-day cycle plan applies this without restating it. Change it here once; every future cycle inherits the change.

**Week runs Sunday → Saturday.** Per week: **2 Articles, 4 Threads, 21 Tweets (3/day, every day).**

---

## The Week Template

| Day | Slot | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| **Sun** | Article A | Anchor #1. Long-form. Opens the week. |
| **Mon** | Thread 1 | Corresponds to Article A (Correspondence principle — the thread is the article's teaching unpacked in public). |
| **Tue** | Thread 2 | Corresponds to Article A — second angle, second entry point. |
| **Wed** | Article B | Anchor #2. Long-form. Opens the second half of the week. |
| **Thu** | Thread 3 | Corresponds to Article B. |
| **Fri** | Thread 4 | Corresponds to Article B. |
| **Sat** | — | No new anchor. Rotation day: tweets recap the week's 4 threads before Sunday resets the cycle. |
| **Every day** | 3 Tweets | Call back to a thread. Never freestanding. |

**Why Sunday and Wednesday:** they split the week 3/4, so each article gets a clean run of days for its two threads to unpack it before the next anchor lands. Nothing competes with itself.

---

## The Correspondence Rule

Every Thread must trace to one of that week's two Articles — stated or implied, the reader should be able to feel which Article a Thread came from. Two Threads per Article, not interchangeable filler: the first entry (Mon / Thu) opens the idea broad; the second (Tue / Fri) goes narrower or answers the objection the first one raised.

Threads are not required to *originate* fresh that week — a strong existing Thread may stand in the Thread-2 slot (re-promoted) if it genuinely corresponds to that week's Article. Mark it `(reprom.)` when reused so the supply is never papered over.

## The Tweet Callback Rule

Tweets never point at nothing. Each day's 3 tweets call back to **the most recently published Thread** at the time they post:

- **Sun / Wed (Article days):** tweets tease that day's fresh Article — the callback target is the Article itself, since no new Thread has dropped yet.
- **Day a new Thread drops:** that day's tweets pull first from that Thread's own tweet-length material.
- **Days between Threads:** tweets continue drawing from the most recent Thread's pool until it's exhausted.
- **Sat (rotation day):** tweets rotate back across all 4 of the week's Threads — whichever got the least air time.
- Across the 30-day cycle, once a pool of Thread-native tweets runs dry, a tweet may reach back into an **earlier week's** Thread — the callback web is cycle-wide ("throughout the weeks"), not boxed into a single week.

## Three Supply Sources (carried forward, unchanged)

21 tweets/week is a real number — filled honestly from three sources, never padded:

1. **New standalone tweets** — purpose-written for a specific Thread.
2. **Thread-embedded tweets** — each Thread already contains tweet-length beats; they count toward volume even when not separately scheduled.
3. **Recirculated archive** — proven, already-published historical posts, requoted on days not otherwise filled. This is the literal mechanism for boosting existing on-platform content rather than only ever posting new.

Where a plan says **"draw from archive,"** that slot is intentionally left open to fill at scheduling time from the real post history, not an invented placeholder.
