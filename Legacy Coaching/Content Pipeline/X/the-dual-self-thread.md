## Tweet 1
There's a version of you that has done everything wrong.

It's still awake. And it has been voting on your life every single day — you've just never once challenged the ballot.

🧵

## Tweet 2
Jung called it the shadow. I call it the Dual Self.

It doesn't confront you. It withdraws. The follow-up you didn't send. The opportunity you let go cold. The retreat you dressed up as "being realistic."

That wasn't wisdom. That was the veto.

## Tweet 3
You can do everything right on the surface — the discipline, the vision, the whole architecture of the life you say you want — and still lose to a part of you that's never once been invited to the table.

## Tweet 4
The fix isn't more discipline aimed at the shadow. It isn't shame. It isn't exile.

All three just teach it to hide better and vote from further underground.

The fix is union.

## Tweet 5

The practice: sit with the version of you that's done everything wrong. Don't argue with it. Don't fix it. See it fully.

Say: "I see you. I accept you. You are mine. You deserve everything that is rightfully yours."

Feel its power return to your body.

How does this change the way you move forward?
