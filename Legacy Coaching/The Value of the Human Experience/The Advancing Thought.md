# THE ADVANCING THOUGHT
### Session One — Why You Forgot You Were Great

*You were never told a lie you couldn't survive. You were told a lie that made you forget you were the one telling it.*

*This is the transmission that hands the pen back.*

**AUKDOM ETHNINE LEGACY COACHING**
*The Value of the Human Experience · Session One · 2026*

---

## I. The Question Behind the Question

Why do you believe you are not amazing? Because someone told you so. Because you decided society failed you before it had the chance to prove otherwise. Because somewhere along the way, not being destined for greatness felt safer to believe than the alternative.

Here is the reframe doctrine opens on: what if all of that was part of the plan?

The journey to greatness is meant to be forgotten so that you can actively remember how truly great you are. Not a design flaw. Not evidence against you. The forgetting is the mechanism, not the malfunction.

> **Reference:** [[Value Content Doctrine]] §I, "The First Claim" — "The forgetting is not the tragedy of the human experience. The forgetting is the plot." This session is that claim, applied directly to one specific belief: *I am not amazing.*

## II. The Advancing Thought

Because the path is designed this way, your remembering does something the forgetting alone never could: it becomes contagious. Your own journey back to knowing your greatness actively inspires greatness in whoever is close enough to watch it happen.

Doctrine names this state the Advancing Thought — living, daily, from the conviction that you are already abundant beyond measure, and letting that conviction be the frame everything else gets read through. Not positive thinking as decoration. A held orientation that the self entering the room is always the self moving forward, never the self standing still.

> **Reference:** Wallace D. Wattles, *The Science of Being Great* and *The Science of Getting Rich* — the "Advancing Thought" is one of Wattles' own named concepts: holding the impression and conviction of increase so consistently that it is communicated to everyone you contact. Worth reading the chapter directly — this session's language is a direct descendant of it.

Wattles' actual work closely enough — including this specific phrase — that I'm citing him by his real, findable name so the reference actually works when you go check it. ## III. Scientifically and Mathematically Abundant

Doctrine does not ask you to take abundance on faith alone. [[The Sovereign Civilization Company]] has already done the math on what the Earth actually makes available once it's organized around remembrance instead of scarcity — commute hours reclaimed, food waste eliminated, yield per square meter multiplied ten to a hundred times over in the right design. The scarcity was never in the resources. It was in the form the resources were forced into.

Call back to that doctrine here, directly: the Earth was never short on what it takes to provide for everyone. The shortage was a story that became infrastructure.

> **Reference:** [[sovereign-city-design-working-document-expanded]] ("The Sovereign City — A Working Document for Urban Form in the Age of Remembrance"), Sections I and V — the diagnosis (commute time, food-mile waste, green-space deficits, all cited with real figures) and the yield math (5–15+ kg/m² per year at the module scale, 10–100x multipliers at district scale). Its closing line: *"The resources were never missing."*

## IV. Multidimensional Capacity

You are a being of multidimensional capacity. You live in a constant state of creation. You are never simply dead or alive — you are only ever living in the moment of your desired choice. Every day you rise is a choice, made again, to be in this life.

This is not a new claim inside this body of work. It is the same claim [[Quantum Freedom]] already doctrines: you exist in superposition across every possible version of yourself, and the self you experience right now is the one you are actively, continuously choosing to collapse into being. Multidimensional capacity is Quantum Freedom, spoken in the register of a first video instead of a first principle.

> **Reference:** [[Quantum Freedom]] §I — grounded there in the double-slit experiment and Hugh Everett III's many-worlds interpretation (1957). Same claim, same evidence, different register.

## V. What Tiredness Actually Signals

If you truly did not want to be here, doctrine holds, that impulse would be the ego talking — not the soul. What reads as tiredness or a lack of fulfillment is, far more often, the soul signaling that its actual purpose isn't yet being lived. Not a verdict on who you are. A signal, asking to be followed rather than pushed back down.

> **Reference:** Cross-reference with the applied-Dispenza thread proposed for Session Five in [[The Eight Remembrances]] — the nervous system as the actual site where unfulfillment registers physically before it's ever named emotionally.

## VI. The Oversoul

Live from the placement of your soul. Let the body be governed by the mind, and the mind be governed by the soul. Let God be God. When you live from this placement — what doctrine calls the oversoul — you remember you were never separate from the intelligent substance of the universe. You are part of the absolute. You are part of the great consciousness. You are part of life because you were always inherently part of it — not a visitor inside nature, but nature itself, briefly self-aware.

Being in tune — with nature, with the body, with the room you're standing in — is not a mood. It is the practiced result of living from this placement instead of from the ego alone. From there, the masterfulness of God's own creation becomes visible, because you are finally standing in the right position to see it.

> **Reference:** Ralph Waldo Emerson, "The Over-Soul" (*Essays: First Series*, 1841) — the specific term and claim (each individual soul as a chamber of one universal soul) traces most directly to Emerson, not Wattles. Surfacing this one myself since it's a strong match to your language here — worth confirming it's the thread you meant.
>
> Also: Wallace D. Wattles, *The Science of Getting Rich*, opening chapter — "There is a thinking stuff from which all things are made, and which, in its original state, permeates, penetrates, and fills the interspaces of the universe." The "intelligent substance" language in this section is close enough to be a direct echo.

## VII. Appreciation Expands

Operating from the placement that we are all amazing and capable of anything, appreciation stops being an occasional mood and becomes the default read on everything in front of you:

- The appreciation of people grows.
- The appreciation of your work grows.
- The appreciation of life grows.

> **Reference:** Wallace D. Wattles, *The Science of Getting Rich*, Chapter 7, "Gratitude" — gratitude is doctrined there as the specific mechanism that keeps a mind in close, harmonious relation with the substance it draws from. Read that chapter directly against this section.

## VIII. Desire Is Proof of Capacity

Last: the desire to create and the capacity to create are the same thing, felt from two different sides.

If you hold the desire to accomplish something — in any shape, any form — you already hold the capacity to do it. The great work you want so badly to do is achievable precisely because you want it that badly. Desires, callings, and feelings are not handed out for nothing. Your desire to become the greatest in whatever you're called to is a God-given gift, proof of a capability that was installed alongside the wanting, not separately from it.

> **Reference:** Wallace D. Wattles, *The Science of Getting Rich* — Wattles' recurring thesis that the desire for more life and more expression is itself the pressure of an inherent faculty seeking outlet, and is therefore evidence of the capacity to fulfill it. Read this section directly against that argument — it's the closest match in this whole session to a specific, checkable Wattles claim.

## IX. Closing Doctrine

Eight parts, one thread: you were never behind, never broken, never too tired to matter. You forgot on purpose, on a design that was always going to bring you back — and every part of you that comes back inspires the next person still forgetting.

Asé.
